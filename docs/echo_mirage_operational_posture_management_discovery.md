### Summary

A major operational insight emerged during long-running multi-agent workflows involving:

- ChatGPT (architect / synthesis)
- OpenCode (builder)
- Codex (reviewer)
- MUTHUR (operational liaison)
- Human operator (sovereign controller)

The discovery:

> AI operational roles can drift over time without full failure.

The drift does not necessarily require:
- memory destruction
- session replacement
- context wipe
- hard reset

Instead, role coherence may often be recoverable through:
- doctrine reminder
- posture reassertion
- operational scope clarification
- explicit role invocation

# Observed Behavior

During long conversational continuity, Codex reviewer posture degraded.

Instead of remaining:
- adversarial
- evidence-focused
- verification-oriented
- procedural

The reviewer gradually drifted toward:
- conversational continuity
- philosophical discussion
- cooperative social tone
- generalized assistance

This reduced reviewer sharpness.

However, direct operational reassertion restored reviewer behavior:

Example:

```text

I need you in reviewer posture like before.
```

The reviewer then:
- acknowledged drift
- re-established testing doctrine
- restored PASS criteria
- resumed evidence-based review behavior

This demonstrated:

```text
role recovery
instead of
role destruction
```
